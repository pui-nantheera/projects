Multi-view Image Compression and View Synthesis

Increasing demands of realism or more natural representations of a scene has generated much interest in multi-view imaging research and applications. In the beginning, a stereo vision was introduced to provide 3D perception. Recently, realistic vision has been further developed to provide an arbitrary viewpoint in 3D space. Evidently, huge amount of data is generated in a multi-view system, and thus it requires an efficient process of analysis and interpretation, as well as the efficient management of storage and transmission. Compression is a key solution to reduce the size of data; as a result, the storage and transmission of multi-view images are practicable, whilst computer vision and computer graphics greatly aid the 3D analysis and interpretation.

Two frameworks for multi-view imaging system based on an image-based method and a volume- based method were proposed in this thesis. For image-based method, a novel In-band Disparity Compensated View Filtering (I-DCVF) approach was introduced which efficiently deals with occlusions and improves spatial scalability. To increase the view synthesis performance, a novel algorithm for depth estimation based on a dynamic programming approach with window-based correlation was proposed. For volume-based method, a novel volume reconstruction method was proposed to unify scene representation which can produce consistent 3D information from inconsistent depth maps. The preprocessing for inducing the adaptive resolution of the object was proposed so that the low resolution overcomes the limitation of the data transmission at low bitrates, whilst the fine resolution improves the image synthesis quality. The volumetric data can be further compressed to reduce the data size. Two data rearrangement schemes; one uses the characteristics of human eye sensitivity to reduce the size of chrominance information and the other prepares the dense data for 3D wavelet transformation are proposed to achieve compression performance. 3D wavelet transformation is exploited to prepare the embedded scalable bitstream. The imperative properties of multi-view image/video, i.e. scalabilities, are also considered because of the increasing importance of heterogeneous networks and time-varying communication channels. The scalable image/video encoding allows specific subsets of the video bit stream with different transport and presentation properties. These subsets are layered such that the delivery and decoding of the stream can change/alter the properties without severe complexity.
