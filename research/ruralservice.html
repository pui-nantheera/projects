Rural/Distributed services and applications

The project is part of theme 1 of the India-UK Advanced Technology Centre of Excellence in Next Generation Networks, Systems and Services (IU-ATC).

Spatiotemporal up-scaling for low bit-rate videos

Remote areas encounter numerous difficulties of telecommunication. Only a small bandwidth is available causing some limitations for video transmission. Therefore, this project proposed a post-processing technique for variable spatiotemporal resolution in which the low-bitrate compressed video can be viewed with desirable display size and smooth motion. The approach comprises a frame rate up conversion (FRUC) and superresolution (SR) techniques for H.264 videos.

Mobile-based disease mitigation system

Most farmers in India are often ill positioned to deal with diseases and plant infestations affecting their crops. Current advisory systems tend to be generic and are not tailored to specific plots or farms. This work comprises an agriculture advisory call centre similar to a modern call centre to provide an agriculture disease mitigation system. The information regarding an individual farm is collected using mobile phones. An image of the diseased/infected crop is also captured using mobile phones and is made available to the expert to provide the advice. To scale the advice, an attempt is also made to automate the disease recognition process using image processing.
